Custom self-study ML program

Since I started getting into advanced analytics, machine learning, and computer science, I have always admired the graduate Computer Science (CS), and Machine Learning (ML), Master's programs at Carnegie Mellon University (CMU). The ML program in particular is consistently ranked as the best ML Master's program in the world and my own self-study has often led me down the YouTube rabbit hole of watching hours of different CMU ML and CS lectures.

I'm too far along in life and have too many responsibilities to pick up my bags and move to Pittsburgh to complete the CMU ML Master's program at this point. However, I know how to self-study and can put together a good study plan and follow it given the right flexibility.

So instead of upending my career or personal life by pursuing a ML Master's at CMU, I decided to put my own curriculum together based on it and fill in a few gaps I think the program has when it comes to solving the last mile problem in ML/Data Science practice—productionizing models and pipelines via the integration of solid CS practices and concepts.

Curriculum
Warmup books

Before launching into the coursework in earnest, I need to ensure my math skills are up to snuff so I am going to work through a few books on key topics.

  • BOOK OF PROOF
  • Lectures on Probability Theory and Mathematical Statistics
  • Linear Algebra: Theory, Intuition, Code
  • Mathematics for Machine Learning

The formal curriculum is a combination of courses from two CMU Masters programs and some additional areas in statistics that aren't deeply covered in the CMU programs.

  • Master of Science in Machine Learning Curriculum
  • Master of Science in Computer Science

The ML program's curriculum makes up the core of this curriculum but it is also supplemented with key courses from CS Master's program.
